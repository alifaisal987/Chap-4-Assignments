In JavaScript, variable names must begin with a letter, underscore (_), or dollar sign ($) and can contain letters, digits, underscores, and dollar signs.
They are case-sensitive and cannot include spaces or hyphens, nor can they be reserved keywords like `class`, `return`, or `null`.
It's also recommended to use descriptive and meaningful names to improve code readability.
